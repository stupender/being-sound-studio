    BESPOKE SOUND

    Sound for your work.

    Original music and soundscapes, composed with the same care you bring to everything else.

    Listen on Substack Book a Discovery Call

    You've made hundreds of intentional decisions about your work. The quality of the light. The language you use. The textures of the room. The rhythm of the experience. The way someone is welcomed in, and the way they are sent off.

    And then, almost as an afterthought, the music. A playlist. A YouTube track. Something from a stock library. Something that was never made for what you do.

    The sound is doing something. It's always doing something. The question is whether it's doing what your work needs it to do, or quietly working against it.

    Brian Eno wrote Music for Airports because the existing airport muzak was designed to soothe and was actually making people more anxious. Ryuichi Sakamoto curated the playlist for the restaurant Kajitsu in New York because the chef had thought about every other detail and the music was undermining all of it. Neither was looking for better music. They were looking for sound composed to belong — to the architecture, the pace, the kind of attention the space was already holding.

    You probably don't need anyone to tell you what good sound feels like. You already recognize it when you hear it, and you recognize when it's missing. My work is to make more of it available for what you're already doing.

    WHO THIS IS FOR

    This work is for people who have already been claimed by sound in some way — moved by it, oriented by it, instructed by it — and who are trying to answer that experience inside their own work.

    You may not name it that way. You might call it: I want the right music for my meditations. Or: my podcast deserves something composed for it, not a stock track. Or: the music in my treatment room should hold what I'm doing for clients, not fight it. Or simply: I know good sound when I hear it, and I haven't found it yet.

    Underneath those requests is something steadier — a recognition that sound is already part of how your work moves, and that the sound you're using doesn't yet match what the work is.

    The form changes. The principle doesn't. I work with bodyworkers, acupuncturists, meditation teachers, yoga teachers, somatic therapists, podcast hosts, course creators, restaurants, retreat centers, galleries, and others whose work asks for sound that belongs to it.

    TWO WAYS TO BEGIN

    There are two ways to work together. The Library is for sound you can use right away. Bespoke Sonic Work is for sound made specifically for your practice, project, or space.

    BESPOKE SONIC WORK

    Sound made specifically for you.

    A bespoke commission is more than a soundscape. It's a sonic artifact that holds an intention, a practice, or a passage you're already inside of — translated into music that belongs to the work it serves.

    Every project begins with a discovery call. The call is part of the work, not preparation for it. The brief you arrive with is rarely the brief that wants to be made, and the conversation is where we find what the work is actually asking for.

    From there, projects generally take one of three shapes:

    • A Single Soundscape — one piece of generative or ambient sound, made for a specific moment in your work. Often a meditation, a class soundscape, or a practice piece.
    • A Sonic Identity — a full theme plus variations, designed to become recognizable as yours. For podcasts, courses, brands, and practices.
    • A Full Commission — a system of pieces working together. For course launches, retreats, podcast seasons, and other projects where sound is woven throughout.

    Pricing and scope are detailed in the Pricing Guidebook, shared after our first conversation.

    WHERE THE WORK COMES FROM

    The sound I make is informed by years of training in jazz (SUNY Purchase, study with John Abercrombie), raga and the Dagar Vani lineage (with Silvia Nakkach), the Bön Five Elements tradition (with Tenzin Wangyal Rinpoche), and zazen practice. I'm not in those lineages as a master. I'm a translator — metabolizing what I've received from these traditions into work that is culturally legible to people who wouldn't enter through their original doors.

    Most of that doesn't need to be made explicit in the work itself. It just needs to be in it.

    MUSIC LESSONS

    A 1:1 space to develop your musical craft.

    Whether you're picking up an instrument for the first time or returning to one you've played for years, lessons are an invitation to grow your musicianship from the inside out — building real craft your hands and ear can actually draw on, deepening your listening, and finding your own voice within the music you love.

    I treat every lesson as a conversation. We start from the songs and sounds that already move you, and use them as the doorway into craft. Theory, technique, repertoire, listening — they all earn their place in service of what you're actually trying to hear, play, or write.

    AREAS OF FOCUS

    • Musical craft and theory, taught in context — not as abstract rules but as the language behind the music you love
    • Instrumental technique and fluency
    • Building a personal repertoire or songbook
    • Deep listening and ear training
    • Improvisation and songwriting
    • Music and recording technology

    WHO THIS IS FOR

    Students of all ages and skill levels — from complete beginners to working musicians refining their craft.

    My primary instrument is the guitar. I also teach ukulele, voice, and music technology.

    If you've been moved by a piece of music and want to understand how to answer it from your own hands, this is for you.

    INVESTMENT

    $100 per hour. Packages, sliding scale, and alternate payment options available upon request.

    ABOUT BEING SOUND

    Being Sound is a sonic arts studio founded by Stu Pender, crafting sonic worlds for artists and cultural builders while cultivating spaces where people gather to listen, learn, and practice together.

    ABOUT STU PENDER

    Stu Pender, Chicago-born musician and sonic artist, weaves elements of improvisation, songwriting, and electronics into orchestrations for the guitar, voice, and laptop.

    A founding member of the hip-hop collective Poetic Thrust, Stu has also worked various roles in the studio with artists Yoh, Cartoons, Topaz Jones, Carlos Niño and Laraaji. Sharing the stage with artists August Ponthier, Photay, Elijah Wolf and Maggie Rogers, he's showcased his talents as an internationally touring guitarist, at The MoMA PS1, United Nations, The Beacon Theatre, and many more.

    Stu’s debut full length album, Stillness In Motion was released in 2021 on the Youngbloods label, earning acclaim from Los Angeles’ KCRW and Apple Music.

    Stu graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in Jazz Studies from SUNY Purchase in 2015. He completed a certificate training for sound in the healing arts with the New York Open Center's Sound & Integrative Music Institute in 2022.

    He currently resides in Brooklyn, New York.
